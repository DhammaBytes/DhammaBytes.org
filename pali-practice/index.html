Pāli Practice

Free app for learning Pāli language grammar, with a focus on verb conjugations and noun declensions. Works fully offline and is available for computers, phones, and tablets.

Install it from the stores:

Google Play App Store

Desktop versions are coming soon!

The app is completely private: no account is required and no data is collected. Everything stays on your device, which unfortunately also means your practice progress does not sync between devices. See details in the Privacy Policy.

For usage tips and frequently asked questions, see the Help page.

Source code is available on GitHub under the CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 license. Word and grammar data are sourced from the Digital Pāli Dictionary project.

About the Icon

The app's icon is the quail from SN 47:6, the Sakuṇagghi Sutta ("The Hawk"), hiding behind clumps of earth in its ancestral territory—a newly plowed field with the soil turned up:

"Wander, monks, in what is your proper range, your own ancestral territory. In one who wanders in what is his proper range, his own ancestral territory, Māra gains no opening, Māra gains no foothold. And what, for a monk, is his proper range, his own ancestral territory? The four establishings of mindfulness."

Icon illustration and app design by Irina Mir.
